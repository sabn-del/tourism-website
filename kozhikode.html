Places to vistit in Kozhikode

Tourism is travel for pleasure and Kozhikode offers many such spots which take you through the past,present and future of the City.

KALLAYI

Kallayi is one of the rivers in Kerala, India. It originates in Cherikkulathur in the Western Ghats, at an altitude of 45 metres (148 ft) and is 40 km (24.9 mi) long. It is linked to the Chaliyar by a man-made canal on the south side of the small timber village of Kallayi lying on its banks.

LOKANARKAV TEMPLE

Lokanarkavu Temple is an ancient Hindu temple situated in Memunda 4 km from Vatakara, in Kozhikode District, North Malabar region of Kerala state of south India. Lokanarkavu is a short form of Lokamalayarkavu which means lokam (world) made of mala (mountain), aaru (river) and kavu (grove).

PARAPALLI BEACH

Parappalli Beach is a surf spot. Parappalli is situated on the rock hills of Kollam beach, near Koyilandy, in the district. There is a mosque near by parappalli beach. Parappalli mosque is one of the most prominent mosques of Sunnis with a history of over 500 years, has become a pilgrim centre for the believers of all communities from around the country and abroad.
